About Us

Our advocacy promotes safer urban cycling by addressing key challenges such as unsafe roads, poorly marked crosswalks, and dangerous intersections. Through dedicated bike lanes, improved infrastructure, and community engagement, we aim to create sustainable and cyclist-friendly cities that benefit everyone.

Objective

Our primary objective is to transform urban areas into safe, accessible, and cyclist-friendly spaces. This includes:

  • Reducing traffic accidents involving cyclists and pedestrians.
  • Promoting the environmental and health benefits of cycling.
  • Encouraging cities to prioritize sustainable urban design.

Projects

1. Cycling Safety Workshops

We organize workshops focused on teaching cyclists safe riding practices, including navigating busy urban streets, understanding traffic laws, and using safety equipment. These workshops are conducted monthly in collaboration with local cycling groups.

2. Infrastructure Improvement Advocacy

Partnering with city planners and policymakers, we advocate for the installation of bike lanes, safe intersections, and cyclist-friendly traffic signals. For example, our recent project in Metro City resulted in the creation of over 10 km of protected bike lanes.

3. School Cycling Promotion

We collaborate with schools to educate children on the benefits of cycling and safety practices. Initiatives include bike-to-school days and installing bike racks on campuses to encourage students to adopt cycling as a primary mode of transportation.

4. Corporate Partnerships

We work with businesses to promote cycling as a sustainable commuting option. This involves setting up bike parking facilities, providing employees with cycling incentives, and conducting safety training sessions for corporate groups.
